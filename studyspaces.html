Find Your Perfect Study Spot at U-M

Discover the Best Study Spaces on Campus

Where productivity meets comfort—your ideal study space awaits!

The University of Michigan’s campus offers countless study spaces designed to meet the unique needs of graduate students. Whether you prefer quiet solitude, a collaborative workspace, or an energizing atmosphere, there’s a spot just for you.

This guide highlights the best places to study on campus based on noise level, helping you find the ideal environment to balance your academic pursuits with well-being. From secluded corners to popular campus landmarks, explore study spots that cater to your preferences—whether you need deep focus, collaborative energy, or a place to recharge.

Here are the types of study spaces:

  • Quiet Spaces
  • Low Noise Spaces
  • Medium Noise Spaces
  • High Noise Spaces

Quiet Spaces

Stay focused and study without distractions.

Hatcher Reference Room:

Deep focus areas with historical vibes, secluded stacks.

Law Library Reading Room:

Strict silence with a grand, academic atmosphere. Ideal for reading or writing.

Tappan Art History Library:

Cozy, with inspiring art books. A hidden gem for art lovers.

Michigan Union Basement:

Comfortable seating with minimal interruptions.

Low Noise Spaces

Minimize distractions, maximize learning.

Duderstadt Center:

Great for group work with modern tech and printers, low hum of conversation.

LSA Building:

Bright, comfortable spaces. Generally quiet with light chatter.

School of Social Work:

Peaceful solo workspaces with snacks available nearby.

East Hall (Psychology Area):

Bright, open spaces, mostly quiet throughout the day.

Medium Noise Spaces

Find balance between focus and collaboration.

Fishbowl (Angell Hall):

Iconic collaborative space with computers.

Michigan League:

Café-style seating with moderate foot traffic.

School of Information Engagement Center:

Vibrant space for group work and brainstorming, with background noise.

School of Information Lounge:

Casual study space with light conversations.

High Noise Spaces

Engage, collaborate, and thrive in a dynamic environment.

Michigan Union (Main Areas):

High-energy social hub for casual tasks.

UGLi First Floor:

The busiest floor, with conversations and activity. Great for collaborative work or if you don’t mind noise.
